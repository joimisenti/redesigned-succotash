Welcome to #.mood!

The How

Navigate to the Colors page to create your own mood labels associated with your own choice of colors.

Visit My Day to visualize your mood, poke around, and satisfy your sensory stimulation for how you might be feeling today.

Head to the Calendar to enter your moods for each day. Look back on your history, see where you've been, and how far you've come.

The Why

This website was inspired by Mood Tracker pages, particularly those originating from Bullet Journals (BuJos).

BuJos subverted the one-size-fits-all approach of the traditional planner. They have a remarkable capacity for customization to suit anyone's individual need, which adapts for neurodivergence and personal preference to help you stay on track of your own organizational goals.

The only drawback that causes many to falter is the time and effort required. This webpage was designed with that in mind.

The Mood Tracker helps to debunk your own cognitive bias regarding your own well-being, to undo that vague idea of "it's been a rough/awful/bad week or month or year." The tracker is an explicit and self-evident bird's-eye-view that you're likely doing better than you think, more good days than bad.

As an extension of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and mindfulness practices, you provide the irrefutable proof from your own documented experience, and you have the most power to change your own mind.
